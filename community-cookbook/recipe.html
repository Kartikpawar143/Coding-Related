Grandma's Apple Pie

By Sarah Johnson
Prep: 45 min Cook: 50 min Serves: 8
4.8 (156 ratings)
Desserts American Baking Apple Pie

About this recipe

A classic American dessert with a flaky crust and cinnamon-spiced apple filling.

The story behind this recipe

This recipe has been in our family for four generations. My grandmother learned it from her mother, who brought it from Germany when she immigrated in the early 1900s. Every fall, we would pick apples together and spend the afternoon making pies.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water
  • 8 cups thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten with 1 tbsp water (for egg wash)
  • 1 tbsp coarse sugar for sprinkling (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1

    In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar for the crust. Add cold cubed butter and cut into the flour using a pastry cutter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. 2

    Gradually add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, stirring with a fork until dough holds together when pressed. Divide dough in half, shape into disks, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  3. 3

    Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice. Toss until apples are evenly coated.

  4. 4

    Roll out one disk of dough on a floured surface to a 12-inch circle. Transfer to a 9-inch pie dish. Fill with apple mixture and dot with butter pieces.

  5. 5

    Roll out the second disk of dough and place over the filling. Trim excess dough, seal and crimp edges. Cut several slits in the top crust for steam to escape. Brush with egg wash and sprinkle with coarse sugar if desired.

  6. 6

    Bake for 45-50 minutes, until crust is golden brown and filling is bubbling. Cover edges with foil if browning too quickly. Cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving.

Notes & Tips

  • For the flakiest crust, make sure all ingredients are cold and don't overwork the dough.
  • A mix of tart and sweet apples gives the best flavor balance.
  • Let the pie cool completely before slicing to allow the filling to set.

Possible Substitutions

  • Apples: You can use any baking apple variety like Braeburn, Jonagold, or Pink Lady.
  • Butter: Vegetable shortening can be used for a dairy-free crust.
  • Sugar: Brown sugar can replace some or all of the granulated sugar for a deeper flavor.
